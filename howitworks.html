ELIGIBILITY

Who can claim a free boiler?

LOW INCOME HOUSEHOLDS

Free and heavily subsidised boilers are being installed in low-income households throughout the UK for those who meet the qualifying criteria. If you are in receipt of tax credits and your income is below £16,010 then you could qualify. In order to move things forward, you will need to complete a simple online application form and we'll be able to put you in touch with an approved, local installer who can assist you further. Someone has to pay for it, right? Boiler grants are funded by the 'big six' energy suppliers who are now obliged to fund the installation of energy saving improvements in to vulnerable homes throughout the UK. The grants have been running for a couple of years now and millions have already been installed.

PENSIONERS

To replace your old, broken or in-efficient boiler you could be looking at costs as high as £4000. However, thanks to the ECO scheme, those who are in receipt of Pension Credit (not to be confused with a standard state pension) could claim a grant to have theirs replaced free of charge, or heavily subsidised. So, who ends up footing the bill? ECO, or the energy company obligation has been set up by the UK government to help vulnerable home owners and private tenants improve the efficiency of their homes and reduce their bills. The non-repayable grants are controlled by the 'Big Six', so that's the energy suppliers including British Gas, EDF, EON, nPower, Scottish Power and SSE.
